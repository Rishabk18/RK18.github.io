NOVICA Amazon Review Analysis

Empowering Artisan Visibility Through Customer Sentiment Intelligence

Problem Statement

NOVICA, an e-commerce platform supporting artisans from developing countries, needed to understand customer pain points across 40,000+ SKUs. The company lacked systematic insight into why certain categories underperformed and how to reduce returns.

Objectives:

  • Analyze 17,000+ reviews to uncover satisfaction/dissatisfaction patterns
  • Detect underperforming SKUs through sentiment analysis
  • Translate findings into actionable business recommendations

Data Collection Pipeline

Built a custom Python scraper to collect Amazon product data and reviews at scale:

ComponentDetails
SourceAmazon product pages via ASIN lookup
Tech StackPython, BeautifulSoup, Requests, Pandas
Output17,265 reviews across 6,973 products

Key Engineering Features:

  • Rotating user agents & proxy support to avoid detection
  • Exponential backoff (5s → 180s) for rate limiting
  • Checkpoint system saving every 10 ASINs to prevent data loss
  • Bot detection handling (CAPTCHA/robot check recognition)
# Core scraping logic
def get_product_page(asin: str) -> Optional[str]:
    url = f"https://www.amazon.com/dp/{asin}"
    for attempt in range(MAX_RETRIES):
        r = session.get(url, headers=build_headers(), timeout=20)
        if r.status_code == 200 and not looks_like_bot_block(r.text):
            return r.text
        # Exponential backoff on 429/503
        time.sleep(min(BACKOFF_MAX, BACKOFF_BASE * 2**attempt))
    return None

Used Car Market Segmentation

Pricing Optimization, Segmentation & Brand Positioning

Problem Statement

The used car market presents significant challenges for dealerships seeking to optimize their operations and marketing strategies. With thousands of vehicles varying across brand, age, mileage, engine specifications, and fuel type, dealerships struggle to make data-driven decisions about three critical areas:

  • Pricing Optimization: Which vehicle attributes have the strongest influence on price?
  • Market Segmentation: Can distinct buyer segments be identified based on vehicle preferences?
  • Brand Positioning: Which brands command premium pricing, and where do opportunities exist?

Price Distribution & Brand Analysis

The price distribution exhibits a classic right-skewed pattern, with most vehicles clustered in the $10,000–$25,000 range. Mercedes, Audi, and BMW command premium pricing, while Vauxhall and Hyundai occupy the budget segment.

Transmission & Fuel Type Impact

Key Findings: Semi-Auto commands highest prices ($24,289), followed by Automatic ($21,603). Manual transmission averages lowest at $12,108. Diesel and Hybrid fuel types command ~$19K, while Petrol averages $14,775.

Price Correlation Matrix

Engine size shows the strongest positive correlation with price (0.64), while mileage has a strong negative correlation (-0.42). Year and price are moderately correlated (0.49).

Recommendations

1. Rebalance Inventory

Shift toward mid-range and economy vehicles where demand is highest (76% of market)

2. Premium Pricing Rules

Add fixed premium for larger engines (+$1K per 0.5L) and automatic transmissions (8-12% higher)

3. Depreciation-Adjusted Rotation

Move older/high-mileage inventory faster with timed discounts

4. Segmented Marketing

Target value buyers with "Low Ownership Cost" messaging; target performance buyers with premium positioning

Conclusion

Data-driven pricing and segmentation create the strongest path to higher margins, faster inventory turnover, and more relevant marketing in the competitive used-car market. Key price drivers are mileage, year, engine size, MPG, and transmission.
